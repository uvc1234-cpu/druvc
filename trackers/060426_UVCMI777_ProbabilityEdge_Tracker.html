🎯 Three Core Ideas from The Shmuts

1. Expected Value (EV) is the only thing that matters. EV = (Win Rate × Avg Win) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss). Positive EV = real edge. Negative EV = guaranteed ruin over time, regardless of psychology.

2. Sample size validates edge. ~300 trades needed for 95% statistical confidence. Anything less is noise — you cannot distinguish skill from luck with small samples.

3. Risk management is survival. Position sizing, Risk of Ruin, and variance management keep you alive long enough for edge to compound. A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain just to break even.

§7 — Risk of Ruin Math

Higher Win Rate Reduces Ruin Risk Exponentially — Not Linearly

This is the most important math in The Shmuts' framework — and the reason UVCMI 777™'s win-rate improvement is a survival mechanism, not just a comfort.

Risk of Ruin Formula

RoR = ((1−W)/W × 1/R)^N
─────────────────────
W = Win Rate
R = Reward:Risk ratio
N = Account ÷ Risk-per-trade
(e.g. 5% sizing → N=20)

This formula governs every trading account. Higher win rate reduces RoR exponentially — not in a straight line.

The Exponential Safety Effect

At 55% W, 1.5R, 5% sizing:
RoR = (0.545)^20
RoR ≈ 0.00054%
─────────────────────
At 70% W, 1.5R, 5% sizing:
RoR = (0.286)^20
RoR ≈ 0.000000001%

The jump from 55% to 70% win rate doesn't halve your ruin risk — it makes it effectively zero. This is why multi-timeframe filtering is a survival tool.
